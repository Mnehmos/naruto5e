TITLE: Day 1 — The Boy With the Quiet Hands
DATES: Academy, Year One · Day 1 of the Hidden Stone · a single day, dawn to dark
---
> The Hidden Stone does not sleep so much as settle — slowly, with great weight, certain it will outlast anything that climbs it. This is the first day a boy named for rock begins to climb.

## 05:30 — The Stoneware House, before the bell
Dawn has not cleared the cliffs. Iwao wakes on the floor-mat to the smell of wet clay — his mother **Tsuru** already at her potter's wheel — and before anything else he does the thing she taught him when he was small and frightened of his own hands. He sits. He breathes. He pulls one clean thread of each nature up through the Ram seal and simply *holds* it: not to do anything, only to know it, so that it never does anything he did not ask.

The thread comes up earth-brown, wind-pale, ember-red, and it does not so much as tremble. *(Morning Chakra Control — natural 19, total 24 vs DC 10. Effortless.)* Across the room the wheel slows for half a breath. Tsuru watches the back of her son's head and says nothing, and her shoulders are very still. **Control was never the problem.** Control comes free. It is everything the control is *for* that frightens the people who love him.

## 06:00 — Breakfast, and the sparkly hands
Mai — six years old and a sensor in the cradle — has felt the glow from the next room and arrives demanding *the sparkly hands.* Iwao obliges over rice, threading light between his fingers while she shrieks. His father **Gennai** leaves for the quarry grey at the cuffs, knocks his son's shoulder once — *"Hidden Stone made you; don't let it spend you"* — and is gone into the grit-light. Tsuru watches a beat too long. *(Family, dawn — Mai's bond deepens; the house is, for one more morning, only a house.)*

## 08:00 — First Period: Chakra Control (Tetsuna-sensei)
A single dry leaf on every desk. *"Channel one nature and cut it,"* says Tetsuna, who is built like a retaining wall and disapproves of erosion. *"One clean cut. Most of you will mangle it."*

Iwao sets three fingers to the leaf and threads not one nature but three, in sequence — a slice of **wind**, a sear of **fire**, a fold of **earth** — and the leaf falls in four pieces where the class was asked for one. *(Chakra Control — total 22 vs DC 14. Clean.)* Silence. Tetsuna's stone face runs the calculation it will run all year: *how much gift, how much boy.* In the doorway **Ryūen-sensei**, who keeps inventing reasons to stand near this class, murmurs it like a man naming the dead — *"Earth, Wind, and Fire. In a child."* At the back, **Ganryū's** pencil snaps in his fist. *(The Academy's ledger turns a page — iwa_academy standing +2.)*

## 09:30 — Second Period: Taijutsu & Conditioning
And here the god-touched hands mean nothing. Tetsuna runs them up the inner cliff-stair under full packs — pure leg, pure lung, no chakra tricks. Iwao's hands are a marvel; his legs are eleven years old and entirely ordinary. He comes in near the back, palms on knees, lungs full of stone-dust. *(Athletics — total 9 vs DC 12. Failure.)* Up ahead **Ganryū** crests the stair first, barely winded, and turns to watch the heir straggle — and something curdles in his face, because he has just watched the universe hand a leaf-cut to someone for free and make *him* climb for the same thin air.

## 11:00 — Midday, the yard
Ganryū finds him at the posts. *"The wind cut that leaf. Not you."* He is not shouting, which is worse. *"Get used to earning things, heir."* Iwao does not argue — and his silence, to a boy who climbs for everything, lands like pity. *(Ganryū's regard for Iwao drops; the rivalry takes its first breath.)* Round-faced **Akemi** drops down beside Iwao anyway and talks enough for both of them, the way she will all year. **Tobari** wordlessly halves a rice-ball. The class becomes, for an hour, people.

## 13:00 — Third Period: History & Strategy (Ryūen)
*"Who can name the bloodline that ground an army to dust at the Battle of the Border?"* Iwao's hand is up before he decides to raise it, and the answer pours out whole — the lineage, the seals, the way **Dust Release — Jinton** folds three natures into a frame of pure dissolution and lets nothing inside it remain a thing. *(History — total 24 vs DC 13.)*

And in the reciting comes the cold stone that will sit under his ribs all year: *the man who did this is not dead. He is the Third Tsuchikage. He is alive, above us, right now — and the whole village keeps searching my face for his.*

## 14:30 — Fourth Period: Bukijutsu (Kanna-sensei)
Coin-sized target, far wall. *Hit it once by week's end,* Kanna says, *and earn a merit mark.* Iwao throws on the first breath and buries the shuriken dead center. *(Dexterity — total 18 vs DC 13.)* The week's mark, earned on Day One, first throw. Not a fluke — a **fact.** His hands are a gift twice over. Kanna writes something down and says nothing. Three posts over, Ganryū throws until his knuckles split.

## 16:00 — Sensei-sanctioned training (Ryūen)
Ryūen keeps him after, and pointedly will *not* let him reach for the bloodline. *"Small natures first. In the hands, before the blood. Show me wind."* He sets a straw dummy at the range, and Iwao molds his first real ninjutsu — **Wind Release: Sickle Weasel.**

The technique tears off his fingertips in a spiral of cutting wind and the straw figure simply comes apart — twenty-eight points of slashing on the dice, of which the dummy needed only eight to become confetti. *(Cast resolved: C-rank, 8 chakra; hit AC 9; the dummy is destroyed. Chakra 14 → 6 — one technique and his reserve is nearly half gone.)* This is the shape of Iwao on Day One: **a cannon with a thimble of powder.** Ryūen watches the confetti settle and does not look delighted. He looks like a man who has just confirmed something he was afraid of. *"Enough. That's plenty of wind for one boy in one day."*

## 19:30 — Dinner at the Stoneware House
Gennai comes home grey with dust and washes to the elbow, and over the stone-pot stew Mai blurts all of it — the leaf, the shuriken, *the wind that ate a dummy.* The table goes quiet. His father sets down his bowl with the care of a man who handles heavy things for a living.

*"A bloodline isn't a crown, boy. It's a heavier stone. The only question that's ever mattered about a heavy stone is whether the man carrying it crushes the people walking next to him."* He holds his son's eyes a long moment. *"So don't."* *(Gennai — a defining beat; the spine of everything that comes after.)*

Later, washing bowls, Tsuru finally says the thing she has held all day: in the market they spoke her son's name in the same breath as the Tsuchikage's, *and he is eleven.* She makes him promise to come straight home tomorrow. It is not a question. *(Tsuru — defining; a mother bracing against a current already pulling.)*

## 22:30 — Sleep, and the eye above
Mai curls against his side. The Stoneware House ticks and cools. Iwao presses his gifted, frightening hands flat to the floor and lets the day go. *(Long rest — chakra 6 → 14, the Will of Fire renewed.)*

He does not know the village moves around him while he sleeps. He does not know that on the highest terrace a small, stooped figure stands with his hands behind his back, looking down through the dark at one particular window; that the headmaster **Sōun** spends a quiet word on his behalf; that **Ryūen** sits late over a thirty-year-old field report. *(World tick, magnitude medium: Ōnoki watches over Iwao — iwagakure standing → 2; Sōun and Ryūen advance him — iwa_academy standing → 4.)*

"Earth, Wind, and Fire," the old man says to the wind, which takes it and scatters it like everything else. "So. It skips to the boy." He does not sound pleased. He does not sound displeased. He sounds like a mountain, settling — and not at all certain that, this once, it wants to.

Below, the heir of the Hidden Stone sleeps on, dreamless, and does not yet know he has begun to climb.
