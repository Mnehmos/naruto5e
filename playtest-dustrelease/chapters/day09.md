TITLE: Day 9 — Now You Lead
DATES: Academy, Year One · Day 9 · hour by hour
---
The hardest part of trust, Iwao was learning, is that it has two ends. He had spent a week learning to be led. Today he had to learn the other thing, which was somehow worse: to be *trusted,* and to be worthy of it.

## 05:34 — Steadier
The morning molding came easy and stayed easy, the new sturdiness in him holding the three threads like a wider hand holding the same coin. He was, by the small honest measures the body keeps, stronger than the boy who'd reached for the bloodline behind the shed and nearly lost a piece of himself. He noted it without pride. Pride was for things you were given. This he had run a thousand cliff-stairs for.

## 06:06 — The Lunch She Packed
Tsuru pressed a fuller lunch on him — she had decided, somewhere in the last week, that feeding the village's worry was at least something a mother could *do* — and Mai demanded the sparkly hands and got them, and the chip of grey stone went into his pocket and the day began the way every day began, which he had stopped finding boring and started finding precious.

## 07:00 — The Reversal, Promised
"Today you lead," Akemi reminded him on the climb, and tied her own headband down over her eyes right there on the switchback, trusting him to walk her up a cliff-stair she couldn't see. His stomach dropped. It was one thing to be the blind one — the only life at risk was his own. To *lead* the blind one was to hold a life that wasn't his in hands he had spent eleven years being afraid of.

## 08:00–11:00 — The Long Morning of the Eval's Shadow
Classes ran under the new weight of the mid-term: chakra control with a grader's edge, conditioning with everyone pushing a notch harder, history that nobody could concentrate on. Twelve days, now eleven. He moved through it half-present, the other half turning over the problem that actually frightened him — not the test, but the *team.*

## 11:12 — Leading Blind
The empty lot behind the cistern. Akemi blindfolded, hand out, waiting on his voice. And Iwao discovered that leading was the inverse of his whole nature: he could not *control* her into safety, could only *describe* the world honestly and trust her to move through it. *Two steps. There's a lip, lift your foot. Good. Stop—* He read the broken ground carefully, called it clean and plain, and she moved on his word the way he had learned to move on hers, and he brought her ten steps across a field of hazards without a stumble, his heart in his throat the entire way. When she pulled the blindfold off she was grinning. "See," she said. "You can hold something without crushing it. You just have to *say true things and let go.*" He would think about that sentence for years.

## 12:30 — He Chooses His Cell
At lunch he did the second hard thing. He found **Tobari** — quiet, watchful, the boy who shared his rice without being asked — and instead of building the perfect cell in his head and assigning everyone their place, he *asked.* "The eval's in teams. Will you be in mine? You and Akemi. I'm — bad at this. The team part. But I want to learn it with people I trust, not people I can manage." Tobari looked at him a long moment, and then said, in the most words Iwao had ever heard him use at once, "You stopped trying to do everything yourself. Okay. I'm in." Suzume, eavesdropping with her eternal kunai, snorted — but it was an approving snort.

## 13:00–16:00 — A Cell, Practicing
They drilled together through the afternoon — clumsy, mismatched, a cannon and a sensor and a quiet patient shadow — and they were *bad* at it, and Iwao made himself sit in the badness instead of solving it. He asked Akemi where she needed him instead of telling her where to be. He let Tobari set the pace of their movement, because Tobari's patience was a kind of timing Iwao didn't have. By the third run they were less bad. Less bad, he was learning, was how everything good started.

## 19:30 — Dinner
"You're quiet," his father observed, which from Gennai was an invitation. "I'm trying to learn to need people," Iwao said, and Gennai chewed on that the way he chewed on everything, slow and thorough. "That's the heaviest stone there is," he said finally. "Heavier than the bloodline. A man can carry the bloodline alone. He cannot carry a village alone. Best you crack your back on the small weight now, where dropping it only costs you a grade."

## 21:25 — The Plan That Was Really Care
By lamplight he built the cell's eval plan, and caught the old instinct rising — the urge to script every contingency, to make Akemi and Tobari into reliable variables — and this time he set the pen down, and rebuilt it as a list of *what each of them was best at* and *where each of them would need the others.* It was a worse plan, by the cold measure of control. It was a better plan, by the only measure that mattered. He left gaps in it on purpose, for his teammates to fill. Leaving gaps felt like learning to breathe out.

## 22:45 — A Message Left in the Dark
He slept. On the terrace the old man, his long watch over the seed concluded, left word down the quiet chain — not a command, just a question, the kind a Stoneward's questions never quite are: *when the mid-term comes, I will want the heir's marks on my desk. All of them. Including the ones the examiners don't think to grade.* Below, in a house that smelled of clay and stone, a boy slept beside his sister, having spent the day learning the one technique the whole village needed him to master and none of them knew to teach: how to hold something precious without closing his fist.
