DATES: the eastern road · Day 94 · the geometry comes off the page

The village's answer to a warning it didn't fully believe was to send the warning's author to walk the next caravan. Kazan called it prudence. Iwao understood it for what it also was: a test of the boy's eyes, paid for with a caravan's cargo, on a road he'd already said was a blueprint for murder.

So when the rain came, he was on point, and he was ready, and he was right.

## The Road Under Rain
Eight wagons, a dozen guards, the syndicate's nervous factor riding middle, and the Particle art-heir walking the front like a held breath. The rain came grey and steady off the mountain and turned the eastern road to a ribbon of running mud. Iwao kept the frame the whole way — three threads up in an open hand, doing nothing, waiting — and watched the country he'd read in the histories scroll past exactly as the diagrams promised: the high side rising into cover, the low side falling into a gully, the bend ahead where a road stops being a road and starts being a trap.

He'd told them. They'd come anyway. Now the trap had a caravan in its teeth, and the only thing standing between the teeth and the wagons was a boy who had failed to convince anyone but a desperate captain.

At the bend, the rain ate the world, and the bend bit down.

## THE BELL — Round One
*And here we go — they hit at the blind bend, just like the boy read it. Three road bandits boiling up out of the gully, and behind them — oh, that's not a bandit. That's a blade. A hired one. Fast.*

[OOC · AMBUSH. Iwao (L6, 38 HP, AC 12) vs 3× Road Bandit (AC 10, 20 HP, +11) and **Ren the Severed Coin** — elite rogue-nin, AC 14, **50 HP, +20 to hit.** Initiative: Iwao 18, Ren 15, bandits 13/11/6. Iwao's up first.]

Iwao doesn't flinch and he doesn't go wide. He picks the dangerous one — the blade, not the boys — and opens his hand at it. The Particle art comes off his palm clean and aimed, the Honed Detachment, a scalpel of unmaking reaching for the rogue's center.

*And Ren is GONE — look at that, he pours off the line like water, the Particle art catches a sleeve, an edge, half of what it reached for—*

[OOC · Iwao → Honed Detachment at Ren. Ren **DEX save 22 vs DC 15** — made it; half damage. Rolled 5d8 = 23 → **11 force. Ren 50 → 39.**]

The blade answers, and the blade is *quick.* Through the rain, inside Iwao's reach before the Particle art has finished settling, and the cut lands across the heir's ribs — a hot bright line, the worst hit he's taken since a stadium and a stronger boy.

[OOC · Ren attacks Iwao — **23 vs AC 12 (nat 16), hit. 15 slashing. Iwao 38 → 23.**]

*And a bandit piles on while the heir's reeling—*

[OOC · Road Bandit → Iwao — **18 vs AC 12, hit. 8 slashing. Iwao 23 → 15.** The other two bandits break for the wagons.]

Twenty-three damage in a single exchange. Iwao's at fifteen, bleeding in the mud, a faster blade in front of him and the wagons screaming behind. A year ago he'd have panicked and clenched and made a fist of the whole bloodline and probably won and definitely terrified everyone watching. He's not that boy anymore. He breathes. He holds the frame.

## Round Two — The Frame, Not the Fist
*Heir's hurt, he's bleeding, and the blade's gonna come again — what's he do, does he go for the kill—*

No. He does the thing a thinker does when he's slower than the man trying to kill him: he doesn't trade. He *cages.* Iwao drops his hand and the earth of the killing-bend — the very ground that was shaped to murder him — comes alive at his word and tries to close over the rogue like a fist of the world itself.

*Sand Coffin! He's trying to bury the blade in the trap they built for HIM — and Ren — Ren slips it AGAIN, this man is made of rain—*

[OOC · Iwao → Sand-Coffin Binding at Ren. Ren **DEX save 20 vs DC 15** — slips the bind; half. But the crushing earth rolled huge: 5d8 = 41 → **20 earth. Ren 39 → 19.**]

The bind doesn't hold — Ren's too nimble to entomb — but the closing stone still crushes the breath and half the fight out of him on the way past. And now the arithmetic of the thing turns over in the rogue's quick, mercenary head, plain as a ledger: he is at nineteen, the boy keeps not dying, the Particle art has come for him twice and twice nearly caught him, and he was hired to make wagons disappear, not to die on a muddy bend fighting the one thing in Hidden Tor he was specifically told to avoid.

*And the blade's looking at the gully — ladies and gentlemen, I think the hired help just remembered he works for money—*

Ren broke. One look at the heir standing bloody and unbothered in the rain with three natures open in his hand and not a trace of panic in him, and the fast blade made the only smart play a paid man can make: he cut his losses and went over the low side into the gully and the grey, and his bandits — paymaster gone, two of them nursing dust-scored arms — broke after him. The ambush came apart not because Iwao crushed it, but because he would not break, and a thing that cannot break is not worth dying on.

[OOC · Ren disengages and flees per his mercenary directive ("cut your way out and live to spend the coin"); the bandits break with their paymaster. Final: Iwao 15/38, bloodied but standing. Caravan held — one wagon lost in the first rush. No kills by the heir's hand.]

The caravan was mostly saved. One wagon had gone over in the first rush, its cargo scattered in the mud. The guards were shaken and two were cut and all of them were alive. And Iwao stood in the thinning rain with his ribs on fire and understood that he had just won the way the old man on the terrace had been trying to teach him to win since a cavern and a natural one: by holding, by lasting, by being the frame that does not give — and by leaving a man alive to run, instead of a thing unmade in the road.

## What the Rain Was Hiding
He almost missed it, because winning hurts and the rain hides everything. But Iwao read the battlefield the way he read everything, and the battlefield, like the bend, had been *arranged.*

[OOC · Aftermath, Perception 16 vs DC 13 — success.]

The bandits had fought hardest — stupidly hard, hard past the point of profit — for one particular wagon. And in the mud beside it, dropped where it would survive a fire and a flight and be *found,* lay a thing that did not belong: a blade-token in the unmistakable script of the Hidden Cloud, the kind of personal marker a Cloud shinobi carries and never, ever leaves behind. Too clean. Too convenient. Lying exactly where a grieving village would look.

Iwao crouched over it with the rain running off his nose and felt the whole shape of it land at once, the way the bend's geometry had landed. *This was never a robbery.* The wagons were the stage. The dead — and there were meant to be dead, his among them, the famous heir cut down on the eastern road — were the message. And the message, written in a planted Cloud-token, said: *Cloud did this. Cloud killed your children's hero on your own road.* Someone was not stealing from Hidden Tor. Someone was trying to hand it a war, gift-wrapped, with the Cloud's name forged on the card.

He picked the token up out of the mud before any of the shaken guards could see it and tuck it into the story it was planted to tell. He did not yet know whose hand had laid it. He knew, with the cold clean certainty that had replaced the lie of the sunrise in his chest, exactly what it was *for* — and that he was now carrying, in a closed and bleeding hand, the most dangerous thing in the eastern country: the proof that the proof was a lie.

## Behind the Wall
He came home in the dark, soaked and stitched and quiet, and did not show his family the token, and did not sleep much. Up on the terrace the old man received a report that the heir's caravan had been hit at the bend exactly as warned, that the heir had held and driven off a hired blade without a single kill, and that he had come back with something closed in his fist that he would not put in the report. Vohl sat with that a long time.

*He was right about the road,* the Stoneward thought, *and he survived being right, and he is carrying something he is afraid to write down.* He tapped the cane once on the stone. *Tomorrow the boy will have to decide who to hand a war to. Let us see if the one who cannot be read can also keep a thing he should not yet share — or whether he is still young enough to trust the wrong adult with it.*

The mountain settled. Somewhere to the east a fast blade counted his coin and his luck. And somewhere closer, a patient man heard that his clean little war had come home bloodied and a wagon short and *seen* — and began, without heat, to plan a version the heir could not simply pick up out of the mud.
