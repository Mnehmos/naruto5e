Day 8 — The Body Catches Up by Inches

Growth is the only jutsu nobody can see being cast.

← Previous Index Next →

Growth is the only jutsu nobody can see being cast. It happens in the dark, in the boring middle of a thousand repetitions, and then one ordinary morning you wake up and the world has quietly resized itself around you.

05:33 — A Longer Breath

He folded the Ram before the light and held the three threads — earth, wind, fire — and noticed, distantly, that the old tremor of fatigue did not come when it usually came. He held the balance a full breath past his limit of a week ago, and then another, and the threads did not gutter. Somewhere in eight days of cliff-stairs and small-nature drilling and blind walks and not-quite-enough sleep, his body had begun, by inches, to catch up to the gift it carried. It was not much. It was everything. He let the threads go and sat a moment, surprised by his own steadiness.

06:05 — The Sturdier Boy

Tsuru watched him eat a second bowl and said nothing, but her hand paused on the wheel. He stood a little differently. The hollow-cheeked, wind-thin look of the first week had filled out a fraction; the boy who had come home swaying at two hit points after one spar looked, this morning, like someone a single blow would no longer fold. She was glad. She was also a mother, and she knew that a body growing sturdy is a body the village will ask to stand in front of sharper and sharper things. She put a third pickled plum in his lunch without comment.

07:00 — "Ten Today"

Akemi met him on the switchbacks. "Ten today," she said, like a coach and a tyrant and a friend all at once, and he found he wasn't dreading it. The dread had been the point, once. Now it was just a thing they did.

08:00 — The Headmaster's Announcement

First period was interrupted by the headmaster himself. Sōun was a narrow, dry man who wore his authority like a coat two sizes too large, and he had opinions about the hype that trailed the heir down every corridor — opinions he aired now, obliquely, by announcing that the Academy's mid-term evaluation would fall in twelve days, and that it would be graded blind where possible, by examiners who did not care whose blood ran in whose veins. He looked, very deliberately, at no one in particular, and everyone understood he meant Iwao. A bloodline buys you nothing in my hall, the look said. Iwao found, to his surprise, that he was grateful for it. It was the first time all week an adult had promised to judge him by what he did.

09:34 — Higher in the Pack

The conditioning stair, and this time he did not finish near the back. Not near the front — his legs were still only legs — but a clutch of places higher than a week ago, close enough that Ganryū, cresting first as always, glanced back and clocked it. The broad boy said nothing. But he ran the last flight harder, the way a man runs when the thing behind him has started, unexpectedly, to gain.

11:08 — The Vanishing Cannon

The midday exercise was stealth — a stalk across the broken ground, instructors as the hunted, the class as a tide of clumsy children snapping twigs. Iwao moved through it like water finding the low places. Quiet had always come easy to him; he was a boy who had spent his life trying not to be noticed by a village that noticed nothing else. He reached the objective unseen and unheard, and a classmate breathed, the cannon can disappear?, with a kind of alarm, as if a mountain had picked itself up and tiptoed. He filed the alarm away. A weapon nobody sees coming, he was learning, frightens people more than one they can.

12:30 — Eval Nerves

Lunch was loud with the evaluation. Twelve days. Tobari worried quietly into his rice; Suzume sharpened with more focus than usual; even Akemi's chatter had an edge. Iwao listened and did the thing he did, which was to start, without quite meaning to, building a picture of what the test would demand and where each of them was weak — and then he caught himself doing it for the group, not for himself, and stopped, and looked at his friends, and decided that this time he would ask them what they needed instead of deciding it for them. It was, he understood, the entire lesson of Day Five, finally arriving.

13:00 — Strategy

Third period turned, as if on cue, to the theory of small-unit assessment — how examiners read a cell, what they reward, what they punish. Iwao answered well, but more than that he watched Ganryū answer well too, from the other side of the room, and felt the strange new thing again: not envy, not quite respect. The recognition of a rival who was, against all of Iwao's expectations, worth racing.

14:30 — Bukijutsu

Kanna's range. He threw clean, and then spent the back half of the period helping Tobari fix a release that kept pulling left — patiently, the way Akemi had been patient with him — and found that teaching a thing made him understand his own hands a little better. The gift, shared, did not shrink. That surprised him too.

16:10 — Ten Steps

The blind walk. Ten steps, on her word, through a course she'd salted with obstacles. He took them without flinching and without looking, and at the tenth her hand did not catch his sleeve — there was no edge this time, just open ground — and he stood there in the dark, perfectly balanced, having walked ten whole steps trusting that the world was where someone he cared about said it was. He opened his eyes. Akemi was grinning like she'd won something. Maybe she had. "Tomorrow," she said, "you lead, and I close my eyes." His stomach dropped, and he laughed anyway.

19:30 — Pride and the Worry Under It

At dinner Gennai looked at his son a while and said, "You're carrying yourself different." It was not quite a compliment; from Gennai, observations rarely were. "Steadier." Tsuru's hand found her husband's. They were proud, and the pride had the worry folded inside it the way the home and the work were folded into the chip of grey stone on the sill — you could not have one without the other. Iwao understood that he was growing into exactly the thing they feared and hoped for at once, and that there was no version of the future where he stayed small enough to keep entirely safe.

21:20 — Twelve Days

By lamplight he did not study Dust. He studied his friends — their weaknesses, their strengths, the shape of a cell that could pass a blind evaluation — and for the first time the planning did not feel like control. It felt like care wearing a strategist's clothes. Twelve days. He could work with twelve days.

22:40 — The Old Man Shifts His Gaze

He slept. On the terrace the report came up as it came up every night, but tonight the old man received it differently. The thing he had set himself to confirm — that the boy was being shaped well, that the bloodline would not rot in a careless vessel — he had, this week, confirmed. He let that goal close in his chest like a door, quietly satisfied, and behind it a new one opened, smaller and more dangerous: I should like to see him myself. Soon. Before they make me decide what he's for. Ōnoki turned from the rail. The mountain had finished watching the seed. It was beginning, against its own better judgment, to want to watch it grow.
